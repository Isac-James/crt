What is Candle
Range Theory?

CRT is the art of catching the market's tricks. Big players (banks, institutions) fake a move to steal your money — CRT teaches you to see that fake and trade in the real direction.

The core idea 💡

Every candlestick on your chart has a HIGH and a LOW. Think of them as walls. The market will often smash through one of those walls briefly — not because it wants to go that way — but to trick regular traders into bad trades. Then it turns around and goes the real way.

A real-world analogy 🎭

Imagine a magician waves their left hand dramatically (that's the fake move — candle 2 breaking out). While everyone watches the left hand, the trick happens with the right. CRT is learning to watch the right hand instead.

Key Point: CRT comes from ICT (Inner Circle Trader) concepts. It's not magic — it's understanding that the market is driven by big players who need to take liquidity before they can make their real move.

Who moves the market? 🏦

Banks and institutions trade massive amounts of money. To buy a huge position without pushing the price up against themselves, they need sellers. So they fake the price lower to trigger regular traders' stop losses (which creates sell orders). Then they scoop up those sells and push price up. CRT helps you see this happening in real time.

Candles 101

Before you can read CRT setups, you need to understand what a candlestick is telling you. Each candle is a story of a battle between buyers and sellers.

Anatomy of a Candle

🟢 Bullish Candle

Closed higher than it opened. Buyers won this period. The body is green/white.

🔴 Bearish Candle

Closed lower than it opened. Sellers won this period. The body is red/black.

The Wicks are Everything in CRT 🕯️

In CRT, we don't care much about the body color. What matters is the WICKS — those thin lines above and below the body. A wick above means sellers rejected price from going higher. A wick below means buyers rejected price going lower. When candle 2 in a CRT pattern creates a long wick, that's the fake move — the trap.

AMD: The
3-Phase Game

Every CRT setup follows 3 candles. Each candle has a job. Once you understand the job of each candle, spotting setups becomes easy.

A

Accumulation

Candle 1. The market is consolidating. Big players are quietly loading their orders. Mark the HIGH and LOW of this candle.

M

Manipulation

Candle 2. The fake-out. Price spikes outside the Candle 1 range to trap retail traders. Then closes back inside.

D

Distribution

Candle 3. The real move. Price breaks the opposite side of Candle 1's range. This is where you trade.

A Bullish CRT means you expect price to go UP. The fake move goes DOWN first (to trap sellers), then price rockets higher.

Step 1 — The Accumulation Candle: Find a candle at a key support level. Mark its HIGH (red line) and LOW (green line). This is your reference range. Wait and watch what happens next.

Bullish CRT Checklist ✅

  • ✓
    Candle 1 is at a key support level (horizontal support, order block, demand zone)
  • ✓
    Candle 2 wicks BELOW Candle 1's low, then closes back above it
  • ✓
    Candle 2 body closes inside Candle 1's range (not a full breakout)
  • ✓
    Overall market bias is bullish on higher timeframe
  • ✗
    Do NOT enter if Candle 2 closes a full body below Candle 1's low
  • ✗
    Do NOT trade if there is no key level nearby — context required

Bearish CRT Checklist ✅

  • ✓
    Candle 1 is at a key resistance level (horizontal resistance, order block, supply zone)
  • ✓
    Candle 2 wicks ABOVE Candle 1's high, then closes back below it
  • ✓

  • ✓

  • ✗
    Do NOT enter if Candle 2 closes a full body above Candle 1's high — that's a real breakout
Common Mistake: Many beginners confuse a real breakout with a manipulation wick. The rule is simple — if Candle 2's BODY closes outside the range, it's a breakout, not a fake. The body must close back INSIDE.

Entry Methods

Now you can spot CRT setups. But when exactly do you click buy or sell? There are two main approaches — one for experienced traders, one for beginners.

⚡ Model 1 — Candle 2 Entry (Advanced)

You enter as candle 2 is forming the fake wick and reversing. Highest risk-reward but requires fast reactions.

Harder
Better RR
Needs experience
How: Watch for candle 2 to spike outside the range and show a rejection. Enter when the wick starts forming back into the range. Use order blocks or FVGs on lower timeframe for precision.

✅ Model 2 — Candle 3 Entry (Beginner Friendly)

Wait for candle 2 to fully close back inside the range. Then enter on candle 3 when it breaks the opposite side of candle 1.

Easier
More confirmation
Slightly worse price
How: Wait for candle 2 to close. Set a limit order at the open of candle 3 or wait for price to break above/below candle 1's range. Enter with confirmation.

Lower Timeframe Entry (MSS) 🔬

After spotting the CRT on H1, drop down to the 5-minute chart. Look for a Market Structure Shift (MSS) — a point where price breaks a swing high/low and creates a new direction. Enter on the retest of that level. This gives you a very precise entry and a tight stop loss.

Pro Tip: For beginners, always use the Candle 3 entry. Yes, you get a slightly worse price, but you get confirmation — and confirmation is worth more than a few extra pips when you're still learning.

Timeframes &
Kill Zones

CRT works on every timeframe, but not all timeframes are equal. The trick is to use a higher timeframe to spot the setup, and a lower timeframe to enter precisely.

Higher Timeframe → Lower Timeframe Rule 📐

You always spot CRT on a higher timeframe and enter on a lower timeframe. Never the other way around.

Kill Zones — When to Trade ⏰

Kill Zones are specific times when big institutions are most active. CRT setups during kill zones are much more reliable than random hour setups.

🌍 London Kill Zone

2am – 5am EST
Most volatile. London opens and price hunts stops from Asian session. Best for EUR, GBP pairs.

🗽 New York Kill Zone

8am – 11am EST
Second most active. London/NY overlap. Best setups. Works on all major pairs.

🌙 Asian Kill Zone

8pm – 11pm EST
Slower but sets up traps for London. Great for spotting Candle 1 of CRT.

🎯 4H CRT Special Times

1am, 5am, 9am, 1pm, 5pm, 9pm EST
H4 candle closes at these times. Perfect for spotting CRT on H4 charts.

⚠️ Time Zone Tip: These times are in EST (New York time). If you're in Lagos (WAT), add 6 hours: NY Kill Zone = 2pm–5pm your time. London Kill Zone = 8am–11am your time.

Stop Loss &
Take Profit

Knowing when to enter is only half the game. Risk management is what keeps you in the game long enough to win. CRT gives you very clear levels for both.

CRT Risk Management Visual

🛑 Stop Loss Placement

For Bullish CRT: Place SL a few pips below the low of Candle 2 (the manipulation wick). If price goes below that, the trade is invalid.

For Bearish CRT: Place SL a few pips above the high of Candle 2.

🎯 Take Profit Levels

TP1 (First target): 50% of Candle 1's range. This is your guaranteed partial profit. Take it.

TP2 (Second target): The opposite end of Candle 1's range (full range). Higher reward, less certain.

Risk-to-Reward Ratio 📊

CRT setups typically offer a 1:2 to 1:4 risk-to-reward ratio. That means if you risk ₦1,000, you aim to make ₦2,000–₦4,000. Even if you're only right 40% of the time, you still make money. Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on any single trade.

Golden Rule: Always know your stop loss BEFORE you enter. If you don't know where you're wrong, you don't know your risk — and trading without knowing your risk is gambling, not trading.
